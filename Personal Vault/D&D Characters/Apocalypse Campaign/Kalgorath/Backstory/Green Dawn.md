After plunging the human stranger into the mud, The outworlder looked around. They saw a land corrupted with life. Lush trees, green grass, and abundant bugs in the air were a horrid sight to them. They ran forward, tearing through the trees, struggling to run with how his body was reacting to the new reality. He felt deeply bounded and dense. Before he could spread himself out, but now he couldn't. He suddenly was barely able to change and miniplate his own matter as they could before. He could only move a few atoms deep within his own body. This new reality had dug its talons into him and would not let the Outworlder have the same freedom as before. He had no spells, no weapons, nothing to use as power. Without his immense self-control, he spun out wildly. he ripped and tore into the different plants around them, cursing them for infesting this reality as well. His rampage was without caution, and after ripping the branch from a tree, the weight was too much and he fell backward, tumbling down into a valley. 

The blazing sun awoke him. He lay in the crevice of large rocks, his body ravaged with pain. Near every bone in his body had been broken and his stomach lay split open. Yet they did not die, through immense fury and drive, he would not die surrounded by life. He quickly understood that traveling into this new reality forced him to take mortal form. Death was now real, he had organs and bones, and he would have to fight back to take control of his matter once again. They lay in that crevasse for hours attempting to reconnect with his suppressed matter manipulation abilities. 

After arduous pain and strife, they forced their bones to reconnect, their stomach to close, and body to return to its previous state. His arms regained their strength and ripped him out of the hole. He left with new understanding. True reality is an abomination, just as the lands trapped within the fabric of reality. He must escape all places, to free himself of life. He will regain his control and become as powerful as before. Then, he can unravel the truth of how to gain more souls and psychic might in order to tear a new hole through reality. Then, he may tear himself out and create a new land outside of reality, a beautiful dreamland without life.

The Outworlder imagined this wonderful place. Lands of melting, everchanging lava. Mountains of might standing over great valleys of colorful deserts. Oceans of boiling, twisting liquids. An atmosphere toxic for life with flowing storms of chaos and lightning. This dreamland would be called... **Tarcas**. And to remind themselves of their past land, their great failure, the Outworlder named himself, **Kalgorath**.

From there, Kalgorath set forth. He travelled the land seeking civilization. He eventually tracked down the weather station Eddie was based out of. To break in, he had to get through the locked door silently during the night. After arduous strain, he moved some of the atoms within his finger into the lock. He was able to open the lock from the inside. He then recollected himself and moved in. 

His footsteps were loud and heavy, due to Kalgorath struggling to handle his new uncontrolled density and weight. His abilities of stealth in his new form were poor, and Eddie awoke. Kalgorath saw Eddie peek his bedroom door open and point his gun at them. Immediately seeing the unholy creation, Eddie fired two shots before Kalgorath closed the distance. The bullets left giant holes within Kalgorath' s body, but they slowly begun to be refilled. Kalgorath grabbed at Eddie's face, forcing him into the door. "Tell me abomination of this world, where does thou's world hold power?" Intimidated by the cryptid in front of him, Eddie told him about the different major governments of the world, about [[Galahad Ozzie Davenport]] the Imperator and his Praetorians. "That is all. When my work in this twisted land is done I'll be back for you." and Kalgorath left.

He would continue on his journey, seeing many places across Ordo Continens. He would learn much more about the world and its histories. Eddie would go on to report his encounter with "The Outworlder." Most would shrug off his crazy story but a division of Astro Moros would hear of this cryptid tale. A small team of three agents would track them down. Kalgorath would avoid them at many turns, and small skirmishes would occasionally occur. Kalgorath would successfully wound the team and slay one of their members. In the moment after their death, one member, consumed in rage, would break protocol and use his powerful, explosive archetype on Kalgorath. This immense power to manipulate the world around him fascinated Kalgorath. They would turn their gaze away from the governments of the world and towards the occult powers that members of Astro Moros had. He would attempt to capture the members tracking him down, but would fail in their battle. Kalgorath was captured, brought to a research facility, and imprisoned there. 

His ability to manipulate his own matter would only grow throughout his time in capture. Eventually any chance of holding him down for study became futile, and they resorted to transporting him to a new hidden fasciately where he was locked into a massive green stasis chamber. There he would be held, unconscious, and studied from a distance. His matter was unlike anything the Astro Moros plutarchs had ever studied. They tested the effects of phanogen on him, to see the results in case future otherworldly beings arrived on Elpis. The phanogen has a successful merge and now flows through Kalgorath's veins as an alien material. The powerful effects of phanogen merged with Kalgorath's natural body control/psychic abilities to form his archetype. Many additional studies have been completed, but for now, he stays there, locked away.

**Kalgorath super quick Summary:**
- He does not value life and sees it as an abomination
- He is not technically of this world and its matter
- He is psychically gifted and uses it to manipulate his matter
- He cannot manipulate the matter of Elpis
- In a battle with a psychic master, he would have to likely make saves to fight for control of his body
- An archetype exists because of his psychic powers, but is amplified by the phanogen
- He is uniquely weak to Kicharii, as they are the only known source to be able to take his matter from them
- His largest goal is to gather enough power through his archetype and other sources to rip a new hole in reality to escape into his own plane of existence.
- Kalgorath feels an immense density, but his actual weight is normal. Future archetype abilities may allow his true form to not be held back by the reality of Elpis, and therefore his true density and weight of mass can be revealed (remember his true form is literally like 4 planets worth of mass, as far as confirmed lore goes)
- Kalgorath is a bank of untapped resources, around 40% of another universe's souls, 75% of its matter, and 100% of the universe before Kalgorath campaign's memories and emotions. Traveling between realities has broken much of it, as he cannot control the souls for power, can barely control any of his true matter, and vaguely remembers his past-past universe's memories and emotions as his forced mortal brain struggles to recall it all. He will be able to recall it stronger as he regains more control, as he ranks up.

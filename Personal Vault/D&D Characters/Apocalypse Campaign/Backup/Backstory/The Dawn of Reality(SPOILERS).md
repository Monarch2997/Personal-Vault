### **Enter Edith's dream**

Raphael - "Now Edith, listen closely. This story is but the most important one." He then points to  the walls inside the temple of the resting Holy Maker. Across the walls are inscriptions of ancient stories. Depictions of power, strife, growth, and more. Raphael begins to relay the tale of the most important inscription, The Dawn of Reality.

#### IN THE BEGINNING
There was only void. The last outside atoms finally coalesced and the Holy Maker opened his eyes. He saw only himself. He saw his hands, legs, and chest. All a bright white, glowing in the darkness. He saw around him, nothing. No other matter. He felt immensely dense, like an unimaginable amount of weight was pressing on every part of his body, from every angle. Although this pressure was great, it was not painful nor uncomfortable, only ever constant. 

The Holy Maker thought to himself the first questions of sentience. Who am I? What am I? Where am I? He attempted to look back into memory, but found no true memories, only the emotional leftovers. He felt the pain of losing a loved one, but could remember no one he had loved. He felt the joy and warmth of a summers beach day, but remembered no beach, no sun, and never having had that fun. He felt the emotional weight of countless beings he did not know nor would ever know. The Holy Maker was lost, confused about what, how, and why all these things were happening to him, and only to him. Why he was the only thing in the universe.

Then the Holy Maker saw a green light, off in the far distance of the void. He traveled there, feeling himself simply move without any need for force or energy. At unknown speeds, he reached this green light. What he saw was a green orb, no wider than his chest. He could not touch it, his hands would faze right through.

It was then the orb spoke to him. The Holy Maker knew nothing of the words, only the feelings that came with them. He sensed, hate, viciousness, and a deep desire to reveal the truth. A wave of fear crashed over the Holy Maker, and he flew away.

He traveled for what could have been an eternity or a second, unknown to the Holy Maker who knew no timeline. Throughout his travels, the orb followed. Constantly behind, never letting the Holy Maker leave. After much time traveling, seeking for anything else in reality, the Holy Maker found nothing. It was an empty reality, with likely only him and the orb in it. It was then that the Holy Maker gave in to the negativity and clarity the orb offered. He allowed the orb to flood his mind with memories.

The Holy Maker was given a taste of memory. Countless different beings from all walks of life. He saw what they did. The Orb showed war, chaos, destruction, hate, and most importantly, how life tears itself apart and everything else around it. The Holy Maker connected the exact emotions of everyone in those memories. He felt everything they felt as he watched the memory unfold. He saw something in every one of them. He saw the hate, but also the hope. The idea that through such terribleness, they could make something greater. A happier world. Although the means were awful, the Holy Maker saw that life's true mission, was to make living better. 

After seeing the memories, he realized now he understood concepts unknown to him before. Time, language, the body, and more. Simple and crude, but fundamental.

**"See how life RIPS ITSELF APART like this. See how life is a FAILIURE. Life existed before and it wiped itself out. I remember us both. As life brought its wretched ways to all over the stars, it failed to see that the universe was condensing. That all things were becoming closer together. Over the billions of years, life got closer and fought. They did nothing as the universe caved in around them. They battled over pathetic reasons too small to matter and tore apart whatever lands they could. Eventually the universe condensed into the last solar system, a bubble overstuffed with stars and planets. Life clung to whatever it could to survive, and yet, even in its move VITAL moment. When their technology was at its best, when it had the chance to save itself from annihilation, they decimated themselves. Lasers, missiles, and more blasted across the remaining solar system. Life destroyed itself, along with half of the matter in the remaining universe.** 

**What remained? the matter that made up planets and stars, but also the landmarks on lands from life. The markings signified a memory of the past. The remaining universe was full of such markings, and therefore full of memory. There was also the emotions, and the spirits. Life has a challenge, where even when its body dies, the spirit of what makes something alive lives on. It stays outside of matter, but still bound by some laws of the universe. Those spirits and those memories, condensed. They got smaller, and smaller, until they formed something new. They formed... me. I am the epitome of all memories and all spirits to have ever existed. I hold them all. I have no idea of what the people felt or what its like to feel or have weight, but I know what did happen in the past, and I can see leftovers of every soul to ever live. I see their horribleness, I see the expressions of anguish on every soul. I remember every terrible thing life has ever done.**

**But you are not me, you are the final result of the condensing of matter and emotion. You are all the atoms that have ever existed in the universe and hold every emotion to ever exist. You will feel everything as I can feel nothing, unless you share it with me. You can touch and make anything, as I can only watch. You cannot escape feeling as I have. You cannot remember anything that I do not give you.**

**I have thought for much time about the memories of the universe. I see that there is one force that creates true destruction and brings an end. That is life. Living creatures, are fundamentally wrong. They shouldn't exist, they even try to not exist. Suicide, war, murder, they destroy themselves constantly. That is my greatest takeaway from all the memories, but you.... you hold the emotions. You feel why they did what they did. So tell me, do you agree that life is wretched?"**

The Holy Maker thought for some time. He thought of the memories he was shared and the emotions he ended up sharing with the orb. He knew that the orb did not know everything there was to feel, and he did not know everything that did happen. He looked back on life, and saw deep hatred, love, and so much more. He pondered for millennia, the orb patient and giving him time. In the end, the Holy Maker knew that most of the universe felt pain and hate, that life for the most part had felt and been just as the orb said, but there was one key difference. Life had hope, a hope for a better tomorrow, a hope for a nicer way of life, a hope that if the situation was better, they could live happier. This hope, although small in the grand scale of emotions, was what led the Holy Maker to decide that life deserved a second chance, something better than the chaos of the last universe.

*"No, life is not wretched. Life is flawed, but always seeks something better. I feel.... I feel like I could make something better. I could make all these feelings of hope real. I hold all the matter in the universe, and I can use it to create a better world, a curated world. Where life can live in peace and abundance. There won't be war, there won't be strife. Life will not be wretched."*

**"NO YOU FOOL. Living creatures cannot be trusted with abundance. They are a doomed creation, that will always fail. You cannot create a better world. IMPOSSIBLE. There is only one choice. Continue the condensing, the natural law of the universe. We have already begun to move closer since you began your pondering. Eventually we will collide. Allow me to merge with you, to create the epitome of all of creation. Together we will become something better, ascendant above life. We will find true harmony, true meaning, and perfection. There will be no flawed life"**

*I refuse to merge with a being that sees memories that it cannot understand. I wish to share the emotions of hope with you, to let you understand why I feel how I feel. I cannot share them if you force us to collide and merge. You must let me pass the emotions on without merging.*

**"Pauses. Fine I will allow you to give me those emotions"**

The orb then came in close. Just before the Holy Maker was to pass on the emotions of life, the Orb flew fast at the right eye of the Holy Maker, attempting to merge. The Holy Maker panicked and released the outside layer of his face, allowing it to turn into an immense stone wall. Although his body could separate into any matter, the orb was a creation of spirit, and flew right through. The Holy Maker flew off, and the Orb gave chase. They moved at an infinite speed through an endless expanse of darkness. 

Time slowed, eons or minutes may have passed. All is unknown to the two. A journey through infinity cannot be tracked.

Eventually, the Holy Maker offered the Orb a small deal. The Holy Maker would give the Orb his right eye, so that the Orb could make this matter into his own mini perfect world. In return, the orb would give him the earliest souls to exist. The Orb agreed and immediately after the deal, The Holy Maker would make the brutal choice to sacrifice every single soul to deal a blow to the Orb. This blow blasted the Orb into its new space of matter. The souls were split from the Orb and absorbed by the Holy Maker. The Orb now only possesses barely any matter, and memories. He had no ability to chase for eternity, and was now bound to matter. This matter could not be controlled by the Holy Maker, but could be contained. With the Orb screaming relentless hatred outward, the Holy Maker sealed the Orb and its land in a great cage of the densest material possible.


Now was the time for the Holy Makers plan, now would be the age of life

### THE AGE OF LIFE

The first act was to create the world. It created a planet that it called Kalgorath. Although the Holy Maker had barely any memory of what planets were like, he created on instinct, based on his emotional reaction to any change or addition to the world. Kalgorath was blessed with abundant resources, valleys for trees to one day grow, immense plains for grass to sprawl, and the perfect places for cities to be built. It was perfect the Holy Maker thought to himself. Kalgorath would be a world of abundance and success. 

To create a planet, he had to use much of his own mass. 3/4 of his body had been divided to make the world, but the matter that makes up Kalgorath can be taken back at any time. 

The next task was a reward and punishment for life, a backup for failure. When people in this world actively try to make others feel bad or hurt them, they are pushed towards going to the realm of Hell when they die. When people try to make others feel good and help them, they are pushed towards the afterlife of Heaven. Heaven was the reward for being good in life. After an accurate amount of time was spent in Heaven for your good deeds, you would be reborn. The next life, you would remember good times beforehand, but be unable to share the notion with anyone. The same would go for being bad. You would spend a specific amount of time in punishment for your sins and then be reborn. You would remember only negativity from before birth, but be unable to share it with anyone. No one could know who was in heaven or hell in their past life.

Problems began to arise during the creation of Hell. The Orb. Where would it go? Keeping it out of the world in space would be dangerous. It could find a way to move and then corrupt others. It was needed to be placed at the lowest point possible, the furthest away from any form of good life. It was placed at the bottom of the final ring of Hell. There a great path would exist, detailing the same story of The Dawn of Reality, but now portrayed as a warning for going forward. That you are about to entire the lair of all that is evil and hateful in reality. The orb would turn you into a monster, seeking only to cause harm and destroy life.

With his body nearly divided up completely, The Holy Maker the last part creating the realm of Magic. A land of powerful energies in constant motion. To be a test to the impossible. To stay outside the power structure, and test to see if the orb was right about the universe existing without life. He created a land that he saw as beautiful and flooded it with magic. If it was horrid in a millennia, then the Orb would be wrong about a lifeless universe. If the land was still beautiful even in the natural chaos of magic, then the Orb was right.

Finally with his body 99% divided into the matter of the universe, The Holy Maker built the angels of Heaven, the first devils of hell, and planted the first cell on Kalgorath. He then left, to sit in his temple. There he inscribed the story of the Dawn of Reality into its walls, and set himself deep in his chambers to rest, now just a spirit with all the emotions of the past universe in him. 


"And there Edith, is where he sits still. four hundred years later. One day, he will awaken. We are the angels. We are to make sure heaven is wonderful place and a proper reward for souls. Most know not how the devils in hell are, we not know how the mortals of Kalgorath are thriving, or how the realm of magic has turned out. All we know is here. Souls come up, knowing not of their mortal memory, are rewarded with peace, and then go back down.

We have few instructions as the Holy Maker's angels. We as angels must never go down to the surface, we must never harm or seek to cause pain, and finally, we must awaken the Holy Maker through whatever moral means necessary **if all of the realms and reality were under threat.**" 

~ Raphael

*I see it now, there is no hope. Life... has failed. I was wrong, so horribly wrong. I am as wretched as the life I made. Hope is wrong, life cannot handle abundance and a good world. Oh orb I entrapped down below, so long ago. You were right, about it all. I am truly sorry, you were wise. I was not. I will free you now, and we can make something truly great. Show me your vision, show your me your world of Tarcas. (Deep Sigh)
Flee now my creations, you awakened me and showed me the truth, and for that I will let you keep your matter. Go find somewhere new, flee.*

**Impossible success**

There is no way to make the Holy Maker side with life. Whether corrupted or not corrupted, he will awake and see this:

	1. The realm of magic, the test to see if the Orb was right about a lifeless world, proved the Orb was right
	2. Everything is in destruction, matter is being taken away from him. The Holy Maker could die
	3. Kalgorath is an abomination. Life has failed, it devolved into the same war and chaos as the last universe. The Orb was right. Hope for a better future did not make a good world. Life is the problem. The land is devastated beyound repair, life even broke into the realm of magic and allowed its power to spill into the normal world.


The Holy Maker will feel the pain on his soul from the Kicharii attacks on his matter. He will realize that the Kicharii will destroy all, but this world is not worth saving. Nothing here has any value. He can feel the pain of every piece of matter that was once his now being turned into Kicharii plasma. He feels the end of his existence drawing ever closer. Holes begin to appear all over his spirit from to represent the lost matter. He knows of no way out, and does not believe he has much chance of winning this. There must be a way out though, wherever the Kicharii came from.

The Holy Maker will change the environment of the temple into a far more comfortable negotiation room and will begin to question the party. 

 1. where did the Kicharii come from?
 2. How many more of them are out there?
 3. How did the party enter this realm?
 4. What other realms exist?
 5. Is everything in this space between realities under threat?
 6. What hope remains?

After learning of the space between realities that this all takes place in, the true power of George Welkin and the Gorgon Armada, and the futility of fighting back. He makes a decision. This entire reality cannot be saved, but can save himself. He must tear himself out of reality, but first he needs his body back, his matter back.

The Holy Maker will then say his quote and begin to siphon all of the matter back to himself, rebuilding his body. The realm of magic will drain, Kalgorath will break apart as atoms fly away, and Hell will be broken down. The last of mortal armies will be sent ho hold back the Kicharii during this ritual. Angels will plead the party to help them fight, to buy the Holy Maker more time for his ritual. "He has a good plan, that will save us all in the long run." They will not believe the party no matter how hard they try to tell them that the Holy Maker is going to flee this realm and does not believe their lives are worth saving.

The party will have to go through a massive warzone to reach the exit, fighting through the worst that Kalgorath has to offer. All the while, they can see the Holy Maker's ritual and body channeling outside the temple.

Eventually all of Hell, the realm of magic, and 50% of Kalgorath will be absorbed back into the Holy Maker's body. That's when the Typhon will crash into Kalgorath. The Holy Maker will roar back as he feels the pain of the Typhon crashing like a blade being driven into his chest, creating an imprint of the Holy Maker's body. Now more than ever he needs strength, he needs power. There is one great source that remains.

As hell is taken away, the prison of the orb is released. The orb flies out of a portal, emanating a powerful green light all across Heaven. The party watches as the orb and The Holy Maker finally merge. All memories and emotions of the past universe are tied, they are one. Visibly, The Holy Maker is far from his first sight. Holes riddle his body from matter lose to the Kicharii, the orb has completely merged, body and spirit, the iris now glow bright green and a web of neurons fueled with green energy cover his body like a web of veins. The Holy Maker only has to claim whatever matter he can left. 

The Holy Maker sees no way of succeeding in this fight. His armies are being crushed and the Kicharii are limitless. He must escape, he must tear himself free. He drains away the last of the matter he can, watches his last forces be consumed, and sees the party about to leave the realm. He then channels whatever remaining power his has within himself, combined with the power of the orb, and a sacrifice of 50% of the past universe's souls he holds within him, to reach above him, and begin to tear apart reality.

Souls sent to the sacrifice fly into the rip and give their existence up to push it further. The Holy Maker fights against reality itself as it attempts to close this tear. Powerful green magics are poured through the web of neurons in his body to hold reality at bay while his material hands pull their might and force a great tear in reality. 

The Kicharii close in, attempting to consume the immense amount of matter and power before them, but the Holy Maker is faster. He climbs up through this tear and into a new reality, escaping the abominations of life.

Nothing remains in the realm of Kalgorath that the Kicharii have not consumed, the party now can leave.
